Investigating how media professionals worldwide are embracing the digital era

As we face a global media landscape rife with uncertainty and excitement, a comprehensive understanding of this new era and what it means for journalists has never been more urgent. How are journalists around the world keeping pace with the digital revolution?

To answer that question, the International Center for Journalists (ICFJ) launched the first-ever global survey on the adoption of digital technologies in news media worldwide. The State of Technology in Global Newsrooms survey reached over 2,200 newsroom employees from 130 countries. Dive into the data below.

About

The State of Technology in Global Newsrooms was conducted by the International Center for Journalists International Center for Journalists (ICFJ). ICFJ is at the forefront of the news revolution, offering programs that empower journalists and engage citizens with new technologies and best practices. ICFJ worked with Georgetown University’s Communication, Culture & Technology (CCT) program to administer and analyze the study.

Support for the study was provided by Storyful, Google News Lab, and Survey Monkey.

Methodology

The survey was conducted in 12 languages (Arabic, Chinese, English, French, German, Indonesian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Russian, Spanish, and Turkish). It was available for response on the SurveyMonkey platform from February 15 to April 15, 2017. This report displays only the results of the survey that was targeted at journalists, though a separate survey for newsroom managers was also disseminated. A list of the countries that are included in each region can be found in the full report methodology. The full report is available here (and more notes on methodology can be found here).

The data used for this visualizer are based on multiple choice answers directly downloaded and aggregated from our survey of more than 2700 journalists and newsroom managers worldwide. Written-in responses are not included in this visualizer but were part of ICFJ’s broader analysis for the State of Technology in Global Newsrooms report.

Newsroom types were defined as follows:

  • Traditional news organizations disseminate information primarily in the legacy formats of newspaper, television, print magazines, and radio.
  • Digital-only news organizations exclusively publish in an online format.
  • Hybrid news organizations use a combination of traditional and digital formats
